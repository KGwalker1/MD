## üéØ INTRODUCTION & CONTEXT

### Historical Background

- **Part of**: Mahabharata (Bhishma Parva, Chapters 25-42)
- **Setting**: Kurukshetra battlefield before the great war
- **Characters**: Arjuna (warrior/disciple) and Krishna (charioteer/divine teacher)
- **Verses**: 700 verses across 18 chapters
- **Date**: Estimated between 400 BCE - 200 CE

### Literary Classification

- **Smriti literature** (remembered tradition)
- **Philosophical poem** (Dharmic discourse)
- **Synthesis of Vedanta, Yoga, and Samkhya**

## üìñ CHAPTER-WISE ANALYSIS (Important for NET)

### **Chapter 1: Arjuna Vishada Yoga (The Yoga of Arjuna's Dejection)**

**Key Concepts:**

- Arjuna's moral crisis and **Vishada** (dejection/grief)
- Seeing relatives in opposing army
- Refusal to fight - **Kshatriya dharma** conflict
- Symbolism: Arjuna represents the **jivatma** (individual soul), Krishna represents **Paramatma** (supreme soul)

**Important Verses:**

- Verse 1.1: Dhritarashtra's question to Sanjaya
- Verse 1.28-47: Arjuna's arguments against war

---

### **Chapter 2: Sankhya Yoga (The Yoga of Knowledge)**

**Most Important Chapter for NET**

**Key Teachings:**

1. **Atma is immortal** (Verse 2.17-25)
   - "Na hanyate hanyamane sharire" (Atma is not killed when body is killed)
2. **Sthitaprajna** (Man of Steady Wisdom)
   - Definition: One who has conquered mind and senses
   - Characteristics mentioned in verses 2.54-72
   - **Not affected by happiness or sorrow**
   - Lives a life of **non-attachment**

3. **Karma Yoga introduced**
   - "Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana" (2.47)
   - Right is to work only, never to fruits of work

4. **Yoga defined**: "Yogah karmasu kaushalam" (2.50) - Yoga is skill in action

**Previous Year Questions:**

> **Q. (2018)** Who said, "The Gita teaching stands not for renunciation of action but for renunciation in action"?
>
> - **Answer: (b) Tilak** (Bal Gangadhar Tilak - "Gita Rahasya")
> - Other options: Hiriyanna, Aurobindo, Radhakrishnan

### **Chapter 3: Karma Yoga (The Yoga of Action)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Nishkama Karma** (desireless action) vs **Sakama Karma** (desireful action)
- **Lokasamgraha** (welfare of the world) - 3.20, 3.25
- Everyone acts according to their **Svabhava** (nature)
- **Prakriti** performs actions, not the Atma

**Important Verses:**

- 3.5: No one can remain without action even for a moment
- 3.19: Perform action without attachment
- 3.42: Sense objects ‚Üí senses ‚Üí mind ‚Üí intellect ‚Üí Atma (hierarchy)

**Previous Year Questions:**

> (2) A Karmayogi works for heaven
> (3) A Karmayogi works for the renunciation of all the karma
> (4) A Karmayogi works for lokasamgraha
>
> - **Answer: (c) (1) and (4)** - Works for liberation and welfare of world

> **Q.** What is the essence of Bhagavad Gita?
>
> - **Answer: (c) Karma Yoga**

### **Chapter 4: Jnana Karma Sanyasa Yoga (The Yoga of Knowledge and Renunciation of Action)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Varnashrama Dharma** - division of society based on Guna and Karma
- **Avataravada** - Divine incarnation doctrine (4.6-8)
- **Yajna** (sacrifice) philosophy
- **Different types of sacrifices** (4.24-33)

**Important Verses:**

- 4.7-8: Krishna's promise to descend whenever Dharma declines
- 4.34: Approach the guru with humility (Pranipata, Pariprashna, Seva)

---

### **Chapter 5: Karma Sanyasa Yoga (The Yoga of Renunciation)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Sankhya and Yoga are not different** (5.5)
- Both lead to the same goal
- **Karma Sanyasa** (renunciation of action) vs **Karma Yoga**
- **Dhyana Yoga** introduced

**Important Verses:**

- 5.18: Panditah sama-darshinah (wise see Brahman equally in all)

---

### **Chapter 6: Atma Samyama Yoga (The Yoga of Self-Control/Dhyana Yoga)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Ashtanga Yoga** elements mentioned:
  - Yama, Niyama, Asana, Pranayama, Pratyahara, Dharana, Dhyana, Samadhi
- **Yogi** defined as one who has controlled the mind
- **Dhyana** (meditation) techniques
- **Equality of mind** (Samatva)

**Important Verses:**

- 6.5-6: Uddharet atmanatmanam (Lift yourself by yourself)
- 6.35: Mind control through practice and dispassion (Abhyasa and Vairagya)

**Previous Year Questions:**

> **Q.** According to Bhagavad Gita, how many types of Yoga are mentioned in the text?
>
> - **Answer: (d) Four** (Karma, Bhakti, Jnana, Dhyana/Raja Yoga)

---

### **Chapter 7: Jnana Vijnana Yoga (The Yoga of Knowledge and Realization)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Para and Apara Prakriti** (Higher and Lower Nature)
- **Four types of Bhaktas** (devotees):
  1. **Arta** (distressed)
  2. **Jijnasu** (inquirer/seeker of knowledge)
  3. **Artharthi** (desirer of wealth)
  4. **Jnani** (wise/knower) - considered best (7.17-18)

**Important Verses:**

- 7.19: After many births, the wise surrender to Krishna

---

### **Chapter 8: Akshara Brahma Yoga (The Yoga of the Imperishable Brahman)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Brahman, Adhyatma, Karma, Adhibhuta, Adhidaiva, Adhiyajna**
- **Paths after death**:
  - **Archiradi marga** (path of light) - leads to liberation
  - **Dhoomadi marga** (path of smoke) - leads to return

**Important Verses:**

- 8.5-8: Remembering Krishna at death leads to Him

---

### **Chapter 9: Raja Vidya Raja Guhya Yoga (The Yoga of Royal Knowledge and Royal Secret)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Bhakti Yoga** emphasized as supreme path
- **Prakriti** as Krishna's lower energy
- **Jivatma** as higher energy
- **Surrender** (Sharanagati) as the means

**Important Verses:**

- 9.26: Patram pushpam phalam toyam (offering of leaf, flower, fruit, water)
- 9.32: Even women, Vaishyas, and Shudras can attain liberation

---

### **Chapter 10: Vibhuti Yoga (The Yoga of Divine Glories)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Vibhutis** (manifestations of divine glory)
- Krishna describes His various manifestations in the world
- **Examples**: Among Pandavas - Arjuna, among mountains - Meru, etc.

---

### **Chapter 11: Vishvarupa Darshana Yoga (The Yoga of the Universal Form)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Vishvarupa** (cosmic form) shown to Arjuna
- **Time (Kala)** as the great destroyer
- **Bhakti** as the means to see the cosmic form

**Important Verses:**

- 11.32: "I am Time, the great destroyer of the world"

---

### **Chapter 12: Bhakti Yoga (The Yoga of Devotion)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Two types of devotees**:
  1. Those who worship the **Saguna Brahman** (with form)
  2. Those who worship the **Nirguna Brahman** (formless) - considered more difficult
- **Characteristics of a Bhakta** (12.13-20):
  - Free from hatred
  - Friendly and compassionate
  - Free from "I" and "mine"
  - Equal in happiness and distress
  - Satisfied with whatever comes

**Previous Year Questions:**

> **Q.** Which is not a teaching of Bhagavad Gita?
>
> - **Answer: (b) All egoistic desires should not be directed to God**
> - (Actually, Gita teaches directing all desires to God - Ananya Bhakti)

---

### **Chapter 13: Kshetra Kshetrajna Vibhaga Yoga (The Yoga of the Field and the Knower of the Field)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Kshetra** (field/body) vs **Kshetrajna** (knower of field/Atma)
- **20 qualities** of knowledge (13.7-11):
  - Humility, non-violence, patience, honesty, etc.
- **Samkhya metaphysics**:
  - Prakriti and Purusha
  - **Gunas**: Sattva, Rajas, Tamas

---

### **Chapter 14: Gunatraya Vibhaga Yoga (The Yoga of the Division of Three Gunas)**

**Most Important for NET - Psychology/Philosophy**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Three Gunas** (Qualities of Prakriti):

| Guna       | Characteristics               | Effect                       | Food Type                |
| ---------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| **Sattva** | Purity, knowledge, brightness | Leads to happiness/knowledge | Fresh, pure, vegetarian  |
| **Rajas**  | Passion, activity, desire     | Leads to action/bondage      | Spicy, hot, stimulating  |
| **Tamas**  | Darkness, inertia, ignorance  | Leads to delusion/bondage    | Stale, impure, fermented |

- **Liberation** through transcending Gunas (Gunatita)

**Important Verses:**

- 14.26: Bhakti leads to transcendence of Gunas

---

### **Chapter 15: Purushottama Yoga (The Yoga of the Supreme Person)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Ashvattha tree** (tree of Samsara) metaphor
- **Kshara Purusha** (perishable - all beings)
- **Akshara Purusha** (imperishable - Jivatma)
- **Purushottama** (Supreme Person - Krishna) - beyond both

---

### **Chapter 16: Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga (The Yoga of the Division of Divine and Demoniacal Properties)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Divine qualities** (Daivi Sampad): Fearlessness, purity, self-control, etc. (16.1-3)
- **Demoniacal qualities** (Asuri Sampad): Hypocrisy, arrogance, anger, etc. (16.4)
- **Three gates to hell**: Kama (desire), Krodha (anger), Lobha (greed) (16.21)

---

### **Chapter 17: Shraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga (The Yoga of the Division of Threefold Faith)**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Shraddha** (faith) divided according to Gunas:
  - **Sattvic**: Worships Devas
  - **Rajasic**: Worships Yakshas/Rakshasas
  - **Tamasic**: Worships Pretas/Bhutas

- **Food classification** (17.8-10):
  - Sattvic, Rajasic, Tamasic foods

- **Tapas** (austerity) of body, speech, mind

---

### **Chapter 18: Moksha Sanyasa Yoga (The Yoga of Liberation and Renunciation)**

**Summary Chapter - Most Important**

**Key Concepts:**

- **Sannyasa** (renunciation of work) vs **Tyaga** (renunciation of fruits)
- **Five causes of action** (18.13-15):
  - Adhishthana (body), Karta (doer), Karana (instruments), Prithagvidha (various efforts), Daivam (divine providence)

- **Threefold knowledge, action, actor, intellect, fortitude, happiness** - all classified by Gunas

- **Four Varnas** based on Guna and Karma (18.41-44):
  - Brahmana (Sattva), Kshatriya (Rajas+Sattva), Vaishya (Rajas+Tamas), Shudra (Tamas)

- **Yoga of Surrender** (18.66):
  - "Sarva dharman parityajya mam ekam sharanam vraja"
  - Abandon all duties and take refuge in Me alone

**Important Verses:**

- 18.78: "Wherever there is Krishna and Arjuna, there will be prosperity and victory"

## üîë KEY PHILOSOPHICAL CONCEPTS FOR NET

### 1. **Doctrine of Avataravada**

- Divine descent when Dharma declines
- Not reincarnation but manifestation
- Complete control over Prakriti

### 2. **Doctrine of Nishkama Karma**

- Action without attachment to fruits
- **Not inaction** but selfless action
- Inner renunciation, not external

### 3. **Concept of Sthitaprajna**

- Steady wisdom
- Equal vision (Samatva)
- Beyond dualities (Dvandvatita)

### 4. **Lokasamgraha**

- Welfare of the world
- Social responsibility
- Maintenance of social order

### 5. **Svadharma**

- One's own duty
- Better to do one's own duty imperfectly than others' perfectly
- Based on Guna and Karma, not birth (according to modern interpreters)

### 6. **Samatva (Equanimity)**

- Equal vision towards all
- Beyond pleasure and pain
- Key to Yoga
