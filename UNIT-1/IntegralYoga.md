## 1. INTRODUCTION TO INTEGRAL YOGA

### Definition and Nature

**Integral Yoga** (Purna Yoga) is a comprehensive spiritual path developed by Sri Aurobindo that aims at the transformation of the entire beingâ€”physical, vital, mental, and spiritualâ€”through the integration of all aspects of human nature with the Divine .

### Key Features:

- **Holistic Approach**: Integrates body, mind, life, and spirit
- **Evolutionary**: Views human consciousness as constantly evolving toward higher states
- **Transformative**: Not just liberation but transformation of nature
- **Practical**: "Yoga is nothing but practical psychology" - Sri Aurobindo
- **Collective**: Progress benefits all humanity, not just the individual
- **Scientific**: Based on rational principles and systematic observation

### Core Psychological Concerns:

1. Understanding the multi-dimensional nature of human consciousness
2. The evolution of consciousness from matter to spirit
3. Transformation of the lower nature (ego) into divine nature
4. Integration of all levels of being
5. Development of the psychic being (evolving soul)
6. Realization of the Supramental consciousness

### Distinctive Features:

Unlike traditional yoga which seeks liberation (moksha) through ascent to transcendent realms, Integral Yoga seeks **transformation of life on earth** through the descent of higher consciousness into the physical world .

## 4. THE SEVENFOLD CHORD OF BEING

Sri Aurobindo describes the human being as consisting of seven interconnected planes or levels of consciousness :

### The Seven Planes:

| Plane             | Sanskrit            | Nature                  | Function                    |
| ----------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **1. Sat**        | Existence           | Pure being              | Foundation of all existence |
| **2. Chit-Tapas** | Consciousness-Force | Consciousness as energy | Dynamic power of awareness  |
| **3. Ananda**     | Bliss               | Delight/joy             | Essential nature of spirit  |
| **4. Supermind**  | Vijnana/Mahat       | Truth-consciousness     | Direct knowledge            |
| **5. Mind**       | Manas/Buddhi        | Thought/intellect       | Discursive knowledge        |
| **6. Life**       | Prana               | Vital force             | Energy, desire, action      |
| **7. Matter**     | Anna/Prithvi        | Physical substance      | Form, density, inertia      |

### Detailed Analysis:

#### Upper Hemisphere (Divine/Spiritual)

**Sat-Chit-Ananda**: The Divine Trinity

- **Sat**: Pure existence, being, truth
- **Chit**: Pure consciousness, awareness, knowledge
- **Ananda**: Pure bliss, delight, joy

**Supermind (Vijnana)**:

- Truth-consciousness
- Direct, integral knowledge
- Bridge between divine and human
- Knowledge by identity

#### Lower Hemisphere (Human/Manifest)

**Mind**:

- Divided, analytical consciousness
- Reason, intellect, imagination
- Source of both knowledge and ignorance

**Life (Vital)**:

- Vital force, energy, desire
- Emotions, passions, dynamism
- Link between mind and body

**Matter (Physical)**:

- Densest form of consciousness
- Body, form, inertia
- Complete "exclusive concentration"

### The Psychic Being's Position :

The Psychic Being sits **"behind the heart"** - the connecting link between the lower and higher hemispheres, between the evolving surface nature and the eternal Self.

### Detailed Analysis of Each Kosha:

#### 1. Annamaya Kosha (Physical Sheath)

**Definition**: The outermost, densest layer - the physical body sustained by food (anna)

**Characteristics**:

- Made of five elements (earth dominant)
- Lowest vibration of self
- Includes bones, muscles, organs, tissues
- Sustained by food and nutrition

**Psychological Significance**:

- Identification with body causes suffering ("I am fat/thin")
- Transformation begins with awareness: "my body" vs. "I am the body"
- Physical practices (asana, proper diet) purify this sheath

**Health Implications**: Imbalances here manifest as physical disease

#### 2. Pranamaya Kosha (Vital/Energy Sheath)

**Definition**: The energy body composed of prana (life force)

**Components**:

- **Five Pranas**:
  - Prana: Inward moving energy (respiration, intake)
  - Apana: Outward moving energy (elimination)
  - Samana: Balancing energy (digestion, assimilation)
  - Udana: Upward energy (speech, growth)
  - Vyana: Diffusive energy (circulation, nerve currents)

**Functions**:

- Physiological processes (breathing, circulation, digestion)
- Protection and guidance
- Link between body and mind
- Carrier of vitality and dynamism

**Psychological Significance**:

- Prana flows through nadis (energy channels)
- Blockages cause disease and disorder
- Pranayama (breath control) regulates this sheath
- Vital being (desires, emotions) operates here

#### 3. Manomaya Kosha (Mental Sheath)

**Definition**: The mental layer - seat of thoughts, feelings, and emotions

**Characteristics**:

- Functions through sensory organs
- Experiences likes/dislikes, pleasure/pain
- Operates on three levels:
  - Conscious mind
  - Subconscious mind
  - Unconscious mind

**Psychological Significance**:

- Source of mental suffering and happiness
- Requires pratyahara (withdrawal of senses) to transcend
- Transmits information between body and external world
- Controls activities of prana and physical body

**Transformation**: Mental discipline, concentration, mindfulness

#### 4. Vijnanamaya Kosha (Wisdom/Intellectual Sheath)

**Definition**: The layer of higher knowledge, wisdom, and intuition

**Characteristics**:

- Beyond ordinary mind's discrimination
- Direct knowing without reasoning
- Bridge between individual and universal mind
- Intuitive place of knowing

**Psychological Significance**:

- Awareness of higher mind
- Connection to universal consciousness
- Active during deep meditation
- Creates link between conscious mind and universal mind

**Functions**:

- Discrimination (viveka)
- Wisdom (vijnana)
- Direct perception of truth

#### 5. Anandamaya Kosha (Bliss Sheath)

**Definition**: The innermost sheath - the causal body of pure joy

**Characteristics**:

- Connected to superconscious/unconscious mind
- Sense of oneness with all
- Beyond "I-ness" and ego
- State of peace, joy, love
- Highest vibration

**Psychological Significance**:

- Closest to the Atman (true Self)
- Experience of Mukti (liberation)
- Samadhi (absorption) occurs here
- Very few reach this state; requires decades of practice

**Note**: Unlike Vedantic view where this is the outermost covering of Atman, Sri Aurobindo sees the **Psychic Being** as deeper still - the evolving soul behind all koshas.

### Interconnection of Koshas :

- The sheaths are **interconnected and interactive**
- What happens on one level affects all others
- Imbalances start at Manomaya Kosha and amplify
- Health requires harmony between all five sheaths
- WHO definition of health aligns with this holistic view

## 6. THE STRUCTURE OF CONSCIOUSNESS

### The Outer Nature vs. Central Being

Sri Aurobindo distinguishes between:

**Outer Nature (Surface Being)**:

- Physical, vital, mental sheaths
- Ego-centered
- Subject to ignorance and division
- Temporary formations

**Central Being (True Self)**:

- Psychic Being (evolving soul)
- Spiritual Self (Jivatman/Atman)
- Eternal, immutable
- Source of true individuality

### The Ego vs. True Self

**The Ego**:

- Temporary formation within outer nature
- Mental, vital, physical construction
- "I" or "little ego"
- Creates sense of separation
- Utility: Centralizes and individualizes outer consciousness
- Must disappear when true being is discovered

**The True Self**:

- **Jivatman**: Individual self above manifestation; presides over nature
- **Psychic Being**: Evolving soul within manifestation; delegate of the Self
- **Atman**: Universal Self; transcendent and immutable

### Levels of Mind

Sri Aurobindo describes ascending levels of mental consciousness:

| Level              | Characteristic         | Knowledge Type                   |
| ------------------ | ---------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Ordinary Mind**  | Thought-based, divided | Indirect, through representation |
| **Higher Mind**    | Silence, less thought  | Knowing without thought          |
| **Illumined Mind** | Light, vision          | Seeing reality directly          |
| **Intuitive Mind** | Intuition from outside | Knowledge given from above       |
| **Supermind**      | Identity, integration  | Knowledge by identity            |

**The Ascent Process** :

1. From mind to Higher Mind (silence)
2. To Illumined Mind (vision/light)
3. To Intuitive Mind (intuition)
4. To Supermind (knowledge by identity)

### Definition and Nature

**The Psychic Being** (Antaratman, Chaitya Purusha) is the **evolving soul** - the individual spark of the Divine that develops through lifetimes.

**Distinctions**:

- **Soul**: Divine center; eternal, immutable; identical with Origin
- **Psychic Being**: Formed around the soul; evolutionary; grows from life to life

### Characteristics:

| Aspect         | Description                              |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Nature**     | Divine essence in evolution              |
| **Location**   | Deep within, behind the heart            |
| **Function**   | Mediator between Divine and outer nature |
| **Quality**    | Love, devotion, aspiration, surrender    |
| **Growth**     | Develops from small entity to full being |
| **Continuity** | Survives death; reincarnates             |

### The Psychic Entity vs. Psychic Being :

**Psychic Entity**:

- Present in all humans
- Small, point-like
- Hardly noticed initially
- Latent potential

**Psychic Being**:

- Developed through effort and evolution
- Full-fledged presence
- Can be felt as "psychic presence"
- Guides and transforms the nature

### Role in Transformation :

1. **First Instrument**: Primary tool for triple transformation
2. **Mediator**: Between Divine consciousness and outer nature
3. **Guide**: Directs the sadhana (practice)
4. **Transforming Agent**: Changes ordinary nature to divine nature
5. **Horizontal Process**: Works from within outward (unlike spiritual descent from above)

**Key Quote**: "The soul and the psychic being are not exactly the same thing, although in essence they are the same. The soul is the divine spark that dwells at the centre of each being... The psychic being is formed progressively around this divine centre... until... it becomes the conscious sheath of the soul"

### How Psychic Development Takes Place :

- Through self-observation
- Aspiration for divine life
- Receptivity to psychic influence
- Active consent and effort
- Gradual growth over many lives (typically)

## 9. INTEGRAL YOGA PRACTICE (SADHANA)

### The Triple Path

Integral Yoga integrates three traditional paths:

| Path                       | Aspect                 | Method                              |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Jnana Yoga** (Knowledge) | Wisdom, discrimination | Self-inquiry, surrender of ego      |
| **Bhakti Yoga** (Devotion) | Love, devotion         | Surrender to Divine, worship        |
| **Karma Yoga** (Works)     | Action, service        | Selfless action, offering to Divine |

**Integration**: All three practiced simultaneously - knowledge, love, and action united.

### Key Practices:

#### A. Aspiration and Surrender

- **Aspiration**: Yearning for the Divine, for transformation
- **Surrender**: Giving up ego to the Divine Will
- **Receptivity**: Opening to receive higher force

#### B. Rejection and Acceptance

- **Rejection**: Of ego, desire, ignorance, lower nature
- **Acceptance**: Of divine influence, higher consciousness

#### C. Self-Observation

- Witnessing the nature without identification
- Detached awareness of thoughts, feelings, movements
- Essential for psychic development

#### D. Concentration and Meditation

- Focusing consciousness on the Divine
- Ascent to higher levels of mind
- Descent of peace, light, force, ananda

#### E. Invocation of the Mother

- The Mother (Mirra Alfassa) as representative of Divine Consciousness
- "When you call Mother, she does not just come for you alone. She comes for everyone."
- Collective yoga - progress benefits all humanity

### The Process of Sadhana:

**Threefold Method**:

1. **Consecration**: Offering all actions, thoughts, feelings to the Divine
2. **Concentration**: Gathering consciousness toward the Divine
3. **Contemplation**: Dwelling in awareness of the Divine
